Almond Crossiant
A delicate pastry filled with the sweet nutty goodness of almond paste, dusted with powdered sugar, and baked to a golden prefections.

×
  1. 1 Sheet of puff pastry.
  2. 1/2 cup of almond paste.
  3. 1/4 cup of powdered sugar.
  4. 1 egg (for eggg wash).

×

First, preheat the oven to 375°F. Next, roll out the puff pastry and cut it into triangles. Spread a thin layer of almond paste on each trangle. Then, roll each triangle from the base to the tp and curve into a crescent shape. Finally, brush with egg wash and sprinkle with powdered sugar, and bake for 15-20 minutes until it's golden brown.

Sourdough Bread
A community favorite, boasting a crusty exterior and a soft, airy interior, embodying the classic sourdough charm.

×
  1. 1 cup sourdough starter
  2. 1 1/2 cups warm water
  3. 4 cups bread flour
  4. 1 1/2 teaspoons salt

×

First, mix the starter, water, and 3 cups of flour in a large bowl. This shoulld create a sticky dough. Next, add salt, and remaining flour 1 tablespoon at a time until they are fully incorporated. Then, knead the dough on a floured surface for about 15 minutes. Once the kneading is done, place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise for about 5 hours. Finally, shape the dough into a loaf, and let it rest for 30 minutes. When you are ready to bake, preheat your oven to 450°F and bake for about 30 minutes until its golden brown.

Classic Carrot Cake
An homage tradition and familal bonds, enriched with the natural sweetness of carrots and a crunch of walnuts, it holds a special place in his heart as it was his late grandfather's favorite. Every year on his grandfather's birthay, Denzel bakes this cake to honor his memeory and the bond they shared over the love of baking.

×
  1. 2 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 2 cups granulated sugar
  3. 1 teaspoon baking soda
  4. 2 teaspoon cinnamon
  5. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  6. 1 cup vegetable oil
  7. 4 large eggs
  8. 2 cups grated carrots
  9. 1 cup crushed pineapple
  10. 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

×

First, preheat the oven to 250°F. Next, mix together the flour, sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt in a large bowl. Then, stir in the oil and eggs until smooth. Fold in the carrots, pineapple, and walnuts and then pour the batter into a greased baking pan and bake for 30-35 minutes. Make sure you allow the cake to cool before frosting with cream cheese frosting.

Tips
&
Tricks
  • Make sure to measure ingredients accurately and properly
  • Allow eggs and butter to reach room temperature before baking
  • Rotate the baking tray halfway through baking process to make sure for an even bake
